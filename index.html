Concept One

Architecture
of Water

"Armenian stone patterns
reborn as water."

Khachkar geometry — the Armenian cross-stone with its intricate interlace — is redrawn as a water system. The ornamental lines become rivers, canals, flow paths. The rosette becomes a source point. The repeating pattern becomes a regional map of shared water.

Armenia has been carving geometry in stone for centuries. Here, that geometry flows. Same precision. Same depth. Different element.

Visual Form

  • Khachkar line patterns — redrawn as waterways
  • Interlace ornament becomes river delta
  • Rosette centre = source point
  • Grid pattern = irrigation infrastructure
  • Geometric precision throughout

Texture & Material

  • Stone texture meets water surface
  • Engraved line weight — thin, precise
  • White ground dominant
  • Blue lines on white = flowing ornament
  • Apricot used for key nodes only

Composition

  • Symmetrical but not rigid
  • Full-bleed ornament as background
  • Typography sits in the silence
  • Negative space = white water
  • Pattern breaks at borders intentionally

Reference Points

  • Khachkar stones of Noravank
  • Islamic geometric water gardens
  • Alhambra irrigation plans
  • Escher water studies
  • Armenian manuscript illumination

Mood · Concept 1

Ancient & precise Stone flows like water Pattern is the message Sacred geometry Architectural calm Timeless Armenia

Do

  • Redraw khachkar interlace as water networks
  • Use geometric precision — every line intentional
  • Let white space breathe between pattern elements
  • Blue line weight varies with flow volume / importance
  • Keep apricot for source nodes only

    • Don't

  • Don't use photographic stone textures
  • Don't fill the entire poster with pattern
  • Don't use the actual khachkar cross as a logo
  • Don't add gradients to the ornament lines
  • Don't confuse ornament with decoration — it IS the content

    Concept Two

    Water as
    a Bridge

    "Waves as roads.
    Water connects what borders divide."

    The diplomatic concept. Water flows between territories — it doesn't ask for visas. Waves become roads. Rivers become corridors. The visual language is fluid, directional, optimistic.

    AI-generated or illustrated visuals: water surfaces that carry the suggestion of paths, bridges, and flow between nations. Armenia is present as a gentle hint — an ochre shore, a mountainous horizon — not as a flag or symbol.

    This concept speaks directly to the Crossroads of Peace narrative. Water is the connective tissue of the region.

    Visual Form

    • Long horizontal waves = movement, passage
    • Water surface aerial view — abstract map
    • Thin lines suggesting borders dissolving
    • Bridges rendered as water reflections
    • Flow direction always left to right = progress

    AI Visual Direction

    • Prompt language: "aerial water surface, silk road, connections"
    • Colour grade: cool blue dominant, warm horizon
    • No people, no flags, no recognisable landmarks
    • Abstract enough for multiple readings
    • Armenian ochre only in the light source / horizon

    Composition

    • Horizontal format preferred for digital
    • Text floats above water surface — never sinks into it
    • Generous white or pale sky above
    • Water occupies lower 40–60% of frame
    • One strong horizon line throughout system

    Reference Points

    • Hiroshi Sugimoto seascape photographs
    • Olafur Eliasson water installations
    • Aerial river delta photography
    • Silk Road cartography — ancient route maps
    • UN / WEF diplomatic visual language

    Mood · Concept 2

    Open horizon Flow without borders Diplomatic optimism Silk Road water Calm authority Endless connection

    Do

  • Waves as paths — always directional, never chaotic
  • AI visuals should feel painterly, not photographic
  • Armenia suggested through warm light on the horizon
  • Typography sits on the sky, never competes with water
  • One consistent horizon line across all formats

    • Don't

  • Don't show any specific country's geography
  • Don't use stock water photography
  • Don't make waves literal roads with lane markings
  • Don't add flags or national symbols anywhere
  • Don't let it look like a travel brand or airline

    Concept Three

    Mountains
    in Water

    "Water reflects who we are.
    The mountain sees itself in the lake."

    The most poetic and Armenian of the three. Ararat reflected in Lake Sevan — a image that every Armenian knows. The reflection is not a copy. It is a different truth. Water doesn't just carry — it shows.

    The visual language is built entirely on reflection: the real and the mirror image, the above and the below, the solid and the fluid. Geopolitical stability reflected in natural permanence. Dialogue reflected between nations.

    The Armenian identity is absolute here — but it's geological, not political. Mountains are older than borders.

    Visual Form

    • Perfect symmetry — world and reflection
    • Waterline as the central axis of every composition
    • Mountain silhouette (abstract, not Ararat literally)
    • Reflection slightly distorted = dialogue, not echo
    • Typography can appear in the reflection — inverted

    Colour Treatment

    • Upper world: white, pale, airy
    • Reflection: deep blue, saturated, rich
    • The reflection is more vivid than the reality
    • Apricot only in the peak — sunrise on stone
    • Waterline: a single precise horizontal line

    Composition

    • Strict vertical split — top / bottom
    • Waterline sits at exact centre or golden ratio
    • Can be used as diptych: real + reflected
    • Text in upper half = world above water
    • Forum name appears at the waterline

    Reference Points

    • Ansel Adams — mountain reflections
    • Lake Sevan at dawn photography
    • Magritte duality / mirror compositions
    • Arshile Gorky — abstracted Armenian landscape
    • Japanese ink paintings — mountain reflection

    Mood · Concept 3

    Still water, deep truth Armenia eternal Above and below The mountain remembers Reflection as dialogue Poetic authority

    Do

  • Keep the waterline as a sacred horizontal — never break it
  • Let the reflection be slightly more saturated than reality
  • Abstract the mountain — suggest Ararat, don't illustrate it
  • Use the reflection device in all formats — posters, screens, print
  • Apply the concept to text too: inverted type in the reflection zone

    • Don't

  • Don't use a photograph of Ararat — it becomes political
  • Don't add wave distortion — still water only
  • Don't make it a landscape poster — it's a system, not a scene
  • Don't let the reflection look like a mistake or glitch
  • Don't lose the white — reflection needs the white world above it
